Shiro Wet

One of the most popular dishes in the country, this red-hued stew is made from a meal ground from broad beans or powdered chickpeas. Garlic, onions, ginger, and various other spices add flavor and depth. Chopped tomatoes and chili peppers give it its rich color.

price: 60ETB

Chechebsa

This breakfast dish, sometimes also known as kita fir fir, is made by frying pieces of a flatbread (called kita) in a flour-based batter. The fried bread is then mixed with niter kibbeh, spiced butter, and berbere, a deep red Ethiopian spice blend that combines paprika, coriander, and cumin among other spices. It’s served with honey and coffee or tea.

price: 70ETB

Misr wot

The base of this spicy red lentil stew is berbere. Lentils are one of the most abundant crops in Ethiopia. Onions, garlic, and tomatoes are sauteed in niter kibbeh, then combined with slow-simmered red lentils. The buttery, savory dish is served on injera alongside similar stews.

price: 75ETB

Doro wot

Doro wat or chicken stew is rich and red in color. Almost all Ethiopian restaurants make sure that they have this dish on the top of their menus. You can find this dish in Dashen Traditional Restaurant in Addis Ababa. It includes chicken, an array of vegetables, lentils and spices like berbere. It is served with injera as the base along with salads. It is said that the best way to eat wat is without any cutlery and only injera that is faithfully at your side.

price:200ETB

Tera Sega

A brave heart's dish, tera siga is nothing but raw red meat chopped in cubes. While this can make vegetarians feel slightly queasy, it’s quite an adventurous dish for the non-vegetarians. It is eaten wrapped between bites of injera, dipped in mitmita. It is said that the Ethiopians’ love for raw meat dates back to the 16th century when warriors had to eat raw meat so they don’t start fires and get detected by the enemy. Well, it looks like the taste never left their tongues!

price:200ETB

Kitfo

The national dish of Ethiopia, it is primarily made of raw minced beef; it is a popular local dish and is often prepared on special occasions like family or friend’ gatherings. It took birth in the Showa province in southern Ethiopia. Meat in Ethiopia can either be ordered leb leb, that is, warmed, or raw, it’s up to you. Kitfo is blended with a mix of spices called mitmita and nitre kebbeh, the flavored butter, which tingle the tongue and salivary glands. It can also be served with fresh cheese called ayib and cooked vegetables.

price:300ETB
